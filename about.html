About us

Built by artists, for the next generation of studios.

DREMONPRO is the studio software we wish we had when we were learning. We made it for everyone who has spent a Saturday redrawing the same forearm sleeve six times.

Who we are

A small team of artists, engineers and one ex-roadie.

We're a hybrid of working tattoo artists and 3D / AR engineers. Half of the team has been in studios for over a decade. The other half came from games, computer vision and on-device machine learning. We share a couch and an unhealthy amount of opinions about brushes.

What we don't do: hire from outside the craft. Every product decision passes through someone who has tattooed someone else this month.

"If a working artist can't get from idea to skin in this app on a Tuesday afternoon, we haven't shipped it yet."
What we do

One workflow that replaces six.

Most studios run on a stack: Procreate for design, an iPad for sketching, a phone for client comms, sharpies for stencil tweaks, paper for transfer, a printer in a back room, a DM thread for revisions. DREMONPRO collapses this into one app.

Sketch fast. A canvas tuned for tattoo work, with style-aware AI variations that act like a faster sketchbook, not an ink-generating bot. Your hand still owns every line.

Capture in 3D. Proprietary body scanning that uses the LiDAR sensor on iPad Pro and iPhone 15+. Full body mesh in under ten seconds. No markers, no calibration, no rig. Works on every skin tone and body type.

Place in AR. Project the design onto the actual 3D body of your client and let them sign off the placement before the needle ever touches skin. True-scale stencil export rolls out of the same screen.

How we came up with this idea

It started with a forearm sleeve that wouldn't sit right.

One of the founders was working a six-session sleeve in 2024. The client kept asking, mid-session, "is this where we agreed?" Every reference photo was flat. Every Procreate mockup was flat. The actual arm wasn't.

We tried every existing tool. Photoshop on a phone. AR filters built for Snapchat. 3D scanners marketed for orthotics. Nothing was built for tattoo. Nothing handled the curvature of a deltoid or the geometry of an existing tattoo you're working around.

So we did the obvious thing nobody had done: built one. Three months of prototyping with a half-broken iPad Pro and a willing client. A year of model training on consented studio scans. A second year working with a small group of pro artists in Lisbon, Berlin and Mexico City who were willing to break the build daily.

The private beta opens June 17, 2026. If you've ever redrawn the same arm six times, this is for you.

Principle 01

The artist's hand still wins.

AI is a tool, not a co-author. Every line on skin came from a human. We won't ship anything that crosses that line.

Principle 02

Privacy is the floor, not a feature.

Designs and body scans are encrypted on-device by default. No artwork is ever used to train models. Cloud sync is opt-in and fully deletable.

Principle 03

Built on hardware artists already own.

If we needed a $4,000 scanner to make this work, we wouldn't have shipped. Studios bought iPads. We built around iPads.
